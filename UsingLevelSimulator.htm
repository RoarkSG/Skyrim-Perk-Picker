Using the Leveling Simulator (Unedited Version)

The Leveling engine allows you to simulate leveling up, based on a style of play. The purpose of the engine is an attempt to simulate what perks are available to you based on perk skill requirements. For example, if you do nothing but Conjurations spells, when you reach level 100, you will have over 5 perk points to choose from. But you will never be able to choose “Heavy Armor – Armsman (Level 2)”, because it requires a minimum skill of 40 in Heavy Armor, which you haven’t achieved.

The instructions are split into two sections, 1) How to use the simulator, 2) Assumptions I made on how Skyrim works.

Using the Simulator

 

The simulator attempts to mimic level progression based on a playing style. This would be similar to a class, but it really just defines what skills you are going to focus on. The following styles are available, but you can create your own. Each offered style has the following categories:

·         Major Focus: Skills you are going to use all the time

·         Minor Focus: Skills you are going to use some of the time

·         Ignores Focus: Skills you will never use until your major/minor are maxed

This is accomplished mathematically by assigning each skill a Focus level, from 0 to 10, where 0=Ignores and 10=Major Focus. The general idea is that higher Focus skills are more likely to advance when pressing the Advance button. It is a random process, so you will get different results every time you try.

Some things to keep in mind:

·         Selecting a new race resets your level back to 1

·         You can change your playing type on the fly without resetting any perks. The new settings will be applied to future advancements.

Assumptions

 

Since I haven’t played the game, I’m going off of news leaks and speculation. I will update the simulator when I learn more.

·         I’m assuming that some levels are harder to level up than others, but that is NOT a factor for this engine. For example, in Oblivion Restoration and Marksman took forever to level up, while (IMHO) Conjuration was way too fast. My guess is Smithing and Enchanting will take forever to reach 100.

·         I’m assuming that it takes more effort to increase a skill the higher the skill level, i.e. it is more work to level a skill from 49 to 50 than from 19 to 20. The engine currently does NOT factor this when advancing.

·         It has been said that the theoretical limit is somewhere in the 70’s . The math I’m using stops at 71.

·         Supposedly raising a skill from 49 to 50 makes you level faster than going from 19 to 20. Other sites claim 10 points per level. I’m going to use a simple level-gain to experience mapping algorithm to attempt to simulate it, but I’m completely guessing at the numbers. EDIT: This is currently NOT implemented – see below.

·         It is supposedly harder to level up once you reach level 50. There are 2 ways they can do this that I see (The engine uses the first):

o   Require more xp points to level: Skills still level at the same rate, but it takes more xp points to level

o   Skills take more actions to advance a single point: Skills take longer to advance a single point, but the xp/points per level is the same

o   Edit: I found this post which looks reasonable to me (http://www.reddit.com/r/skyrim/comments/m1m4e/trying_to_figure_out_if_starting_skill_bonuses/ ) , so I’m going with the 10/50 approach.
